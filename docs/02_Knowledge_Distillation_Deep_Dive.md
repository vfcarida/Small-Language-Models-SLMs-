# 2. Implementation 1: Knowledge Distillation

Knowledge Distillation (KD) is the architectural technique where the "dark knowledge" of a massive Teacher model is transferred to a reduced Student model. Instead of relying purely on hard labels present in datasets, the student is trained to imitate the continuous probability distribution (the logits) generated by the teacher across its output layers. The error isn't binary; it measures how closely the student's confidence distribution matches the teacher's.

## Mathematical Theory & Challenges

Traditional divergence is measured through the **Kullback-Leibler (KL) Divergence** function. In the context of distillation, a temperature parameter ($T$) is applied to the softmax functions of both models. Elevated temperature "softens" the distribution, revealing how the teacher evaluates incorrect answers, effectively transferring relational subtleties that are invisible in rigid boolean labels.

However, classical KD methods for autoregressive sequence models suffer from a severe distribution mismatch. During training, the student model is exposed to sequences generated under the ideal conditions of the teacher (or the dataset). But during production inference, the student must act upon the iterative sequences *it generated itself*.

### Generalized Knowledge Distillation (GKD)
To solve this, Hugging Face's TRL (Transformer Reinforcement Learning) library introduced **Generalized Knowledge Distillation (GKD)**. GKD forces the student model to be trained based on its *own* self-generated output sequences, utilizing the teacher's feedback specifically on these student-created trajectories.

This process enables the use of the Generalized **Jensen-Shannon Divergence**, offering massive resilience if the SLM lacks the structural capacity to perfectly copy the LLM.

## Practical Implementation Aspects

For corporate banking scenarios, sequence-based distillation requires generation and gradients to be decoupled. Using the `GKDTrainer` optimizes this flow. It is imperative to configure an external `vLLM` server to host the teacher model (e.g., Llama-3.1-70B), avoiding VRAM collapse on the hardware training the student (e.g., Llama-3.2-1B).

### DeepSeek-R1 and Reasoning Distillation
The advantages of this approach include exceptional performance in closed domains and the forced induction of reasoning. The main disadvantage lies in the high computational cost and initial iterative time required to process the probabilities of each generated token, demanding complex infrastructural coordination between servers.

However, models like **DeepSeek-R1-1.5B** prove that reasoning distillation (forcing the emission of explicit chains of thought via `<think>` tokens) from massive LLMs results in success rates in arithmetic reasoning that vastly surpass traditional training methodologies.
